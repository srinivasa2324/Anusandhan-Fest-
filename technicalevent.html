Tired of being penalized for over engineering???

For all those who love to complicate things – start your train of thoughts, and come up with all the bizarre and uncommon ideas and put them together to accomplish the most common task.

GOAL STATEMENT
• Use various forms of energy conversion techniques to accomplish the goal
• A BALL SHOULD FALL INSIDE THE BOX
• To achieve the requirements given in the format of the event

FORMAT
• A ball should fall inside the box of dimension 20cm * 20cm * 15cm (l*b*h)
• The box should be placed 75cm from the model
• A normal tennis ball size can be used
• Maximum number of pitches outside the box will be given maximum points
• The arrangement should have minimum number of 5 steps before completion of final task
• Automatic start and parallel steps will be awarded maximum points. No points will be awarded for manual start
• The entire contraption, while operating, shall fit and remain completely within an imaginary box whose dimensions are 6 feet * 6 feet* 5 feet
• The teams are not provided any walls or tables for the model
• Power supply of 230V / 50 Hz and a flat ground for display
• Necessary tool kits should be brought by the participants

RULES
• The number of members per team is restricted to 5
• Only 1 team member may interact with the model once the evaluation begins. This applies to resetting the model too
• The model must complete the task as described in the goal statement
• Teams must use minimum of 3 energy conversions
• The model must take no more than 15 minutes per run
• A step in the model should be considered as a transfer of energy from one action to another. Identical transfers of energy in succession will be considered as one step
• For example, a set of dominoes falling onto each other will be considered as one step.
• Any loose or flying objects must remain within the set boundaries of the model
• Contestants are responsible for removing their arrangement and any debris left after the completion
• Core committee members reserve the right to disqualify any team violating the rules or indulging in misbehaviour
• Abstract must be submitted on or before Feb 15, 2017 to vision.mech@psgitech.ac.in
• Use of loose electrical connections, flames and hazardous materials in the arena room should be avoided

SETUP
• Setup time before first attempt – 120 minutes
• Number of chances to show model – 2
• Setup time between two trials – 20 minutes
• Second trial given will have point reduction of 10% from the scores obtained

JUDGING CRITERION
• Innovation
• Visibility of steps
• Theme based demonstration
• Continuity without any human intervention
• Recycled/scavenged materials used
• Number of conversions

PENALTIES
• Run Length exceeded
• Human Intervention
• Deviation from the initial design report
